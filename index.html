Idea project.

Photofessional

A mobile application that teaches photographers how to improve their photographs in a given situation. The application intelligently compares the current view through the mobile device’s camera lens with a resource pool of professional quality photos to determine the best possible settings for the photographer to use on a DSLR camera.

Photofessional,

A mobile app that teaches you to take the best possible photo after recommending changes to your DSLR camera’s settings. The best possible settings are determined by comparing the environment captured by the mobile device and a database of photos. Professional photographs and the photographer’s self-reported settings are to be loaded into a database connected to the app. The app would then display the settings on the screen for the DSLR after you take an example photo with the mobile device. Improved settings will allow you to achieve the same quality or similar quality to the professional photographer.

I personally am an amateur photographer and love to take photos, however they never turn out as I want them to because I don’t yet understand enough about the settings on the camera. I am sure the world is full of people like me who don’t have the time to play with the camera settings every day to learn them. We just wish to take the best photo possible without the stress of taking 100 photos with different settings and choosing the best or processing the photo after the fact. Post processing itself can take a long time to bring the colours out or to make sure that the main feature stands out enough so that the photo doesn’t look bland.

The app would allow you to take a quick happy snap of the selected area where you would like your final photo to be. Then the application would compare the photo to other photos in the database and show you five similar photos. You as the user would then select the scene closest to what you envision your ideal photo to look like and the application would then display the settings on the screen of your mobile device for you to copy on your camera. At this point it would still be possible to easily jump between the settings on all five of the similar photos for each of use. This would sadly not guarantee the best possible photo but it would also help you learn how the settings affect the type of photo you take based on the scene and the light available, effectively teaching you how to take the best photo due to the feedback the application gives.

The operation of this app would be very simple:

  • Select the time of day (which affects the assumed level of light around you).
  • Select the type of photo you would like to take:
    • Selfie
    • Group Selfie.
    • Landscape.
    • Landscape with an individual.
    • Landscape with a feature.
    • Night photography.
    • Action photography.
    • Astronomy photography.
  • Take an example photo that will help analyse the environment and photo setting from database of photos.
  • The app searches through its database and returns at least five similar results of photographs that match selected criteria.
  • From the returned results, select a photograph that best represents how you would like the photo to turn out.
  • The app displays the settings on the screen in order for you to change them on your camera.
  • Adjust settings manually on the camera.
  • Take an improved photo as per the settings.
  • Provide feedback on your result within in the app so the algorithms can be improved.

Future plans for this application can be adding camera manufacturer and the model to help you find where the settings are found for specific adjustments on the camera. Adding lens settings could also be an option as a lens can vastly increase the and decrease the quality of photos. Also adding a specific database of photos that you may have a preference to instead of using the provided data. When selecting your own specific list, you must ensure that all the correct data would be added in the settings. The application would only be able to find the photo and return the settings based on user input. The application could also be expanded to select from a detailed list of photographers based on your preference, however this would require a collaboration from multiple specialist photographers in the industry. This application would also have a heavy focus on asking the consumer if they are happy for the developers to use the settings input within the app and the settings they have provided on photo selection to improve the app.

As this application would be Android or IOS it would be available to most with a mobile phone. With regard to software, there are currently many programs that you can use to code Android and IOS on. While I have done limited research on mobile development tools available it appears that there are many to fit every type of application you may with the produce. You could also host a simple database server with photos as the actual size and quality would not have to be the highest as the end user would only be using them to compare to the current environment.(Mobile Application developement tools, n.d.)

If this project was successful, amateur and hobby photographers would be able to greatly improve their skills and understanding of how DSLRs work and how to get the most out of their very expensive piece of technology. It would also help them understand terminology used by professional photographers. Lastly it would ensure they could see the results of their changes firsthand and this would ensure they would understand what each change would actually achieve. This software could also help professionals compare their own photography style with other professionals and help them not only improve but also understand their differences.
